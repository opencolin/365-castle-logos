Sandbox VR is a location-based, full-body VR arcade where groups of up to six people play cinematic multiplayer experiences like zombie shooters, Squid Game, and Stranger Things in a shared physical space.
Sandbox VR is a “holodeck”-style venue: you go to a physical location, gear up with a headset, PC backpack, haptic vest, and trackers, then roam freely in a room-scale arena with your group. It focuses on social, co-op experiences designed for friends, families, and team events rather than solo home VR.
They offer several proprietary titles you can’t play at home, including:
Deadwood series (zombie horror co-op shooters).
Squid Game Virtuals (Netflix tie-in with competitive mini-games).
Stranger Things VR missions where you fight demogorgons and protect Hawkins.
Other sci-fi and fantasy titles like Amber Sky 2088 and Seekers of the Shard: Dragonfire.
A typical visit is around 60 minutes including check-in, suit-up, the 20–30 minute mission, photos, and watching an automatically generated highlight reel. You wear full-body tracking (head, hands, feet) plus a haptic vest so hits, explosions, and interactions are physically felt.
Pricing varies a lot by venue; many locations charge on the order of tens of dollars per person per session, and some users on forums consider locations charging around 80 USD per person per hour expensive compared to alternatives like laser tag. People generally report the guided setup makes it accessible even for complete VR beginners.
