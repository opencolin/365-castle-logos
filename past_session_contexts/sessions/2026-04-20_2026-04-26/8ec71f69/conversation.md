.you is a relatively new generic top‑level domain (gTLD) operated by Amazon Registry and aimed at personal brands, creators, and consumer‑focused businesses.
.you is a generic top‑level domain, similar in status to .com or .online, not tied to any country or specific industry.
It is positioned around identity, personalization, and “putting you first,” so registrars market it for personal sites, portfolios, creators, and client‑focused services.
The registry backend and policy owner is Amazon Registry Services, Inc., which also operates other new gTLDs.
.you was delegated into the DNS root zone in March 2016, but for years was not open to the general public.
The formal launch phases were: Sunrise (for trademark holders) from 2025‑08‑26 to 2025‑09‑25, followed by Landrush and general availability beginning 2025‑10‑06.
As of early 2026, mainstream registrars (Name.com, 101domain, etc.) are offering .you registrations with standard 1–10 year terms.
Registration is open to anyone; there are no special eligibility restrictions stated for ordinary registrants.
Common pitches from registrars: personal branding (yourname.you), creator and influencer hubs, community platforms, and consumer‑focused services where personalization is core.
It behaves like other gTLDs technically: you get normal DNS, can use DNSSEC, and can point it to any hosting or app stack.
Example retail pricing: some registrars list registrations around 30 USD/year with renewals in a similar range, though promos may be cheaper.
You can search and buy .you domains at major registrars like Name.com, Namecheap, 101domain, and others now that it’s in general availability.
